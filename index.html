    This Bed We Made
    Check out the game on steam

    This Bed We Made is a third-person mystery game in which you play as a maid in a 1950s hotel and snoop around strangers' rooms to uncover their deadly secrets.
    Discover what links guests together in this tale of love, heartbreak and murder!

    I joined this project at its first steps as a Programmer, lead by Jonathan Chouinard in July 2020. After Jonathan departure in 2021, I became Lead Programmer, developing most of the game systems and planning the development of the projet's features.
    We were initially working on Unreal Engine 4.25, but we migrated to Unreal Engine 5 when it became available. It was quite a challenge, we handled it pretty smoothly, even though we had some issue with Wwise integration.
    The prog team was always small, we had some interns : Maxime Houle, Tommy Le Hat, Virginie d’Amours-Licatese; and some short contract programmers : Louis Lamontage(Intermediate), Nadiaa Pryschchepa(Senior), Lijun Zheng(Intermediate).
    I enjoyed mentoring interns, sharing my knowledge and experience with them.
    Managing more experienced programmers was also really interesting. Getting as much knowledge as I can, and optimizing their time and talent for the good of the project.

    We put a lot of effort to make This Bed We Made the best experience possible with the small team we had, especially considering most of us were far from being industry veterans ! And looking at reviews, I think we did quite a good job !

  • The Last Frame
    Unity - Puzzle - Online Coop - 3D

    X

    The Last Frame
    Check the game page on itch.io

    Steph, a young adult, returns to her family's mansion looking for what she believes is the family's treasure, but all is not the way she remembered. It seems she will not be alone in her quest to find her family's legacy...

    The Last Frame is a cooperative puzzle game with an asymmetric gameplay. It was created as part of the Ubisoft Game Lab Competition, an annual competition organised by Ubisoft challenging students in groups of 8 to create a game in 10 weeks, with several constraints to fulfil.

    This year's (2020) constraints were:
    - Build a game around the theme "generation"
    - Your game must offer online and LAN services
    - Your game must be cooperative
    - There must be at least one IA element in your game
    - There should be at least one player progression element in your game
    - Your game will be played by the jury for around 10 minutes

    Sadly, due to covid-19 complications, the Ubisoft Game Lab Competition was cancelled on March 16, only 9 days before it was due. We decided to carry on nonetheless and finish our game.

    This game was created by a group of 8 students currently completing a Computer Science Master's degree at UQAC university (Quebec, Canada).

    In this project, my main responsibilities was all network related programming and some gameplay programming

    Team: Dorian ANTOINET, Raphaelle GARCIA, Laura BOUZEREAU, Baptiste REY, Clement RAMAT, Elie ENSUQUE, Clemence PETIT, Victor DELENCLOS
    Completed in : April 2020

  • Bounty Island
    Unity - Party Game - 3D

    X
    ❮ ❯

    Bounty Island
    Check the game page on itch.io

    Bounty Island is a fun local multiplayer game (2 to 4 players) where the goal is to dig and bring back as many chests possible to your team's base. But beware, because you can also dig up bombs! You can also steal the other team's chests, hit them with your shovel to make them drop their chests, throw the bombs at them before they explode and push your enemies into the water.

    Bounty Island was a game created for a game jam around the theme 'bounty'. With only three days at our disposal, we decided to create a multiplayer minigame loosely based on the mine sweeper game. Our main goal was for players to have fun and to find multiple game strategies: protecting your teammate while he digs a chest, following your opponents and stealing their chests at the last moment, targeting only difficult and rare chests...

    In this project, my main responsibilities were :
    - Finding assets
    - Creating animations
    - Chest digging mechanic

    Team: Raphaelle GARCIA, Laura BOUZEREAU, Baptiste REY, Clement RAMAT, Elie ENSUQUE, Clemence PETIT, Victor DELENCLOS
    Completed in : January 2020

  • Orbs
    Unity - Puzzle - 2D

    X
    ❮ ❯

    Orbs
    Check the itch.io page

    Orbs was produced in 24 hours for Extra Jam 3, which is a game jam organized by Extra Life. The theme was "15 items or less".
    Orbs is a simple and colourful looking game with 10 playable levels. Each level has a common objective : to retrieve all the colored orbs with a minimized number of moves. The player can manipulate his own orb with the mouse by adjusting the angle as well as the shooting power. On its way, the orb may encounter gadgets that will make it accelerate, bounce, attracted or even teleport him somewhere else! With each orb absorbed, the player's orb will grow (up to a certain limit) and will be able to aim for larger orbs.
    Orbs makes extensive use of the particle system and Unity physics.
    In this project, my main responsibilities were :
    - Player Controls (Arrow)
    - Physics
    - Some levels' design
    - Orbs absorption

    Team: Dorian ANTOINET, Victor DELENCLOS, Augustin LEO, Clément RAMAT
    Completed in : November 2019

  • Cataclimax 2119
    Unity - Arcade - 2D

    X
    ❮ ❯

    Cataclimax 2119
    Check the itch.io page

    Cataclimax 2119 was produced in 48 hours for Wonderjam 2019, which is a game jam organized by UQAC (Université du Québec À Chicoutimi). The theme was "Climate disaster in 2119: when the end of the world is near!". 3 styles were also imposed on our team: "Arcade", "Management", and "Adventure". We chose to go with an "Arcade/Management" idea. Another restriction was that the game was to be played on an arcade machine, which was interesting because we had to think about the game design differently compared to a "classic" game.
    Here's a short description of the game : in 2019, many Disasters appear in the world. The goal is to stop them before they actually happen. The difficulty gradually increases as time flows : the player must manage his Counter-Disaster charges and choose to abandon some parts of the world in favour of others. The game is over when a region is completely doomed. Starting from the year 2119, the game reaches its peak of difficulty, which means that A LOT of Disasters are coming!
    The player's choices can also be influenced by each region's weaknesses and resistances to each type of Disaster. These must be discovered and used by the player to reach the highest scores!
    In this project, my main responsibilities were :
    - Team Organisation
    - UI
    - Controls
    - Balance

    Team: Dorian ANTOINET, Victor DELENCLOS, Juliette LALMAN, Clément RAMAT
    Completed in : October 2019
    See the itch.io page

  • 0-GUST
    Unity - Stealth - 2D

    X
    ❮ ❯

    0-GUST
    Check the itch.io page

    0-GUST is the result of my very first game jam : the 45th edition of the Ludum Dare. The duration was 72 hours, and the theme was "Start with nothing". My team chose to make a stealth game.
    The goal is, as a small microwave oven named 0-GUST, to escape a disassembly plant. At first, you don't have any ability and you should get caught pretty quickly. So you have to manage to unlock different abilities as you progress through the game!
    As this was our first game jam, we made a significant number of errors related to time management and task allocation/prioritization. This generated a number of bugs that we were not able to fix in time. After the jam was over, we fixed most of the bugs. Furthermore, we chose to leave only one of the abilities we had created in the game in order to simplify the gameplay (we went a bit overboard and created things like an ice-thrower, an EMP or even a flamethrower...). These could have been interesting, but the code architecture as a whole wasn't really adapted to handle them anyway.
    Precisely because we have made so many mistakes, this experience is valuable.
    In this project, my main responsibilities were :
    - Graphical assets
    - Level design

    Team: Dorian ANTOINET, Victor DELENCLOS, Clément RAMAT
    Completed in : October 2019
